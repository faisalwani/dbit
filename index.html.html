Texas DIR DBITS Contract Vehicle

Report Overview

The Texas Department of Information Resources (DIR) Deliverables-Based Information Technology Services (DBITS) contract is a key vehicle for IT service providers in the state. This report summarizes the process for joining the contract and provides a list of current vendors.

Joining the DBITS Contract

The DBITS contract operates on a closed-award model. This means there is no formal "on-ramping" or continuous enrollment process for new prime contractors once the contract has been awarded. The list of vendors is fixed for the duration of the contract term.

Alternative Paths to Participation

While new prime contractors cannot be added mid-contract, there are two viable paths for participation:

  • Respond to a Future Solicitation:

    The only way to become a prime contractor is to respond to a new Request for Offer (RFO) when one is issued by the DIR. Interested parties should monitor the DIR's procurement schedules for future opportunities as current contracts expire.

  • Become a Subcontractor:

    The most immediate way to get involved is to partner with a current prime vendor. This involves forming a business-to-business relationship and working as a subcontractor on Statements of Work (SOWs) issued under the prime's contract. This is a common and encouraged practice.
